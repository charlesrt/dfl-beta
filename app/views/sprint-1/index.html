Sprint 1 experiments

Sprint 1 focused on experiments to test the highest priority assumptions. Using the evidence from research the team will be able to make new decisions on priority, progress certain tickets to development and identify areas for iteration.

Users need to

Understand the logic behind search

Highlight search strings within results to increase result relevancy

Research discussion:

  • Do users understand the relationship between what was searched and the results?
  • What do users assume of search results without highlighting?

Increase visibility of selected filters within search results

Research discussion:

  • Do users understand the faceted view of the results?
  • Do users understand the AND/OR relationship between facets?
  • How do users react to knowing that some relevant results aren't presented under a filtered view because those filters aren't available in the metadata?
  • Do users know how to remove filters?

Advanced search logic for specificity including operators such as AND and OR

Research discussion:

  • Are user familiar with the formatting of advanced search queries using operators?
  • If not, do users understand how they would use them based on the content available?
  • Do users use the quick links or follow the examples given?
Users need

Filtering to match their expectations

Increase usability and visibility of filter options available

Increase clarity of filters being selected/unselected

  • Filters, with data formats
  • Filters, with content types

Research discussion:

  • Starting with data formats, then content types, how do users need to filter their search?
  • Do users undertand the filter sections available?
  • Do users show/hide filter sections as needed?
  • Do users scroll through filter options within sections to find the filter they're looking for?
  • What do users assume of filters sorted by most results first?
  • Do users understand the number of filters selected within a section?
  • Do know how to select and unselect filters?
